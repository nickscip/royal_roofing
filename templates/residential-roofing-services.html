FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What are the best roofing materials for Texas homes? ⌄

In the DFW area, architectural asphalt shingles are the most common choice because they balance cost, durability, and curb appeal. Impact-resistant Class 4 shingles and metal roofing are strong options for North Texas hail, heat, and wind. Tile can also last a long time, but the roof structure must be able to carry the added weight.

How much does a typical residential roof replacement cost in Texas? ⌄

Most standard asphalt shingle roof replacements in DFW commonly fall somewhere around $8,500 to $18,000, but the exact price depends on roof size, pitch, stories, decking condition, material choice, ventilation, and code requirements. A written inspection and estimate is the only accurate way to price a specific home.

Do I need to be home during roof installations or repairs? ⌄

In most cases, you do not need to stay home during the work as long as the crew has safe exterior access, gates are unlocked, vehicles are moved out of the work area, and pets are kept inside. A homeowner or decision maker should be reachable by phone in case the crew finds decking damage or another issue that needs approval.

Does a warranty come with a new roof from Royal Roofing? ⌄

Most professional roof replacements include a manufacturer material warranty and a contractor workmanship warranty. Coverage depends on the products installed, the final scope, and registration requirements, so the warranty details should be reviewed in writing before the project begins.

Does Royal Roofing handle commercial storm damages claims? ⌄

Most roofing companies can inspect storm damage, document visible roof conditions, provide photos, and prepare a repair or replacement estimate. In Texas, a contractor cannot act as a public insurance adjuster on the same project, so the insurance company and any licensed adjuster make claim coverage decisions.

What roofing permits does Royal Roofing handle? ⌄

Permit rules vary by city across Dallas-Fort Worth. For example, Dallas residential permits are used for construction, renovation, and major repairs to help confirm code compliance. Most roofing companies help identify whether a permit is required for your address and include required permit steps in the project plan.
