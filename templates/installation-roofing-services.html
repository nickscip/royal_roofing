FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What types of roofs do you install? ⌄

Royal Roofing installs roofing systems for residential and commercial properties across DFW. Depending on the building and roof design, we can help with asphalt shingles, Class 4 impact-resistant shingles, metal roofing, TPO or low-slope systems, and other roof assemblies that fit the project scope.

Do you provide framing services? ⌄

No. Royal Roofing does not provide framing services. For installation projects, the builder or framing contractor should complete the roof framing first. Once the structure is framed, decked, and ready for roofing, Royal Roofing can inspect the deck, confirm roof details, and install the roofing system.

How long do roof installations take? ⌄

Many standard residential shingle installations take about 1 to 3 days once work begins, assuming good weather and no major hidden deck damage. Larger homes, steep roofs, specialty materials, commercial systems, city inspections, and North Texas weather can add time. Royal Roofing will explain the expected timeline before work starts.

Do you offer commercial roof installations? ⌄

Yes. Royal Roofing can install commercial roofing systems when the scope, access, materials, and schedule are a good fit. Commercial projects may include TPO, metal roofing, modified bitumen, or other low-slope systems based on the building design, drainage, and long-term needs.

Do you install roofs for new construction projects? ⌄

Yes. Royal Roofing can install roofs for new construction projects after the structure has been framed and decked. We can coordinate with homeowners, builders, and contractors to review roof plans, schedule installation, and install the roof system once the project is ready.

What roofing permits does Royal Roofing handle? ⌄

Permit rules vary by city across Dallas-Fort Worth. In Dallas, residential permits apply to construction, renovation, and major repairs, while commercial permits apply to construction, alteration, and renovation work. Royal Roofing can help identify the permit steps needed for your installation and include them in the project plan.
